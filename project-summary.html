Problem #4 — Ticketing System Replacement

The challenge MasterFlow is built to solve.

Master Electronics spends more than $300,000 per year on ServiceNow but uses it mainly for basic ticket management. The platform is more complex and costly than current needs require, and enhancements often require specialized development. The challenge is to create a simpler, more cost-effective ticketing solution that improves usability, visibility, reporting, and flexibility for internal teams — including migration of existing tickets, users, and knowledge, and at least one AI-assisted workflow.

Business baseline

The starting conditions, stated accurately.

Business Impact
  • More than $300,000/year is spent on ServiceNow, primarily for basic ticket management.
  • The platform is highly capable but more complex and costly than current needs require.
  • Common enhancements may require specialized development.
  • Employees must decide the form, category, queue, and ownership before they can ask for help.
  • Incomplete requests create repeated clarification; incorrect routing delays work.
  • Service Team members spend time interpreting and correcting requests.
  • Queue Managers lack a strong, structured improvement loop; Administrators carry heavy configuration and governance burden.
i
Two separate business measures — never combined.

The ServiceNow platform cost baseline ($300k/year fixed licensing) is distinct from operational ticket-resolution spend (parts, vendor, labor per ticket), which is reported separately in MasterFlow Intelligence. Any savings figures are labeled as modeled or estimated.

MasterFlow’s response — remove unnecessary decisions from the employee

The employee describes what is happening; MasterFlow does the rest.

The employee should not have to decide the department, queue, category, request type, priority, form, required fields, knowledge article, or troubleshooting path. They describe the problem in their own words. MasterFlow interprets the request, reuses employee profile context, makes the routing decision, selects the request flow, and gathers what the receiving team needs.

The objective is not to ask only one question. MasterFlow asks one focused question at a time, and keeps asking the right questions until the receiving team has what it needs to begin work — minimizing unnecessary effort while maximizing work readiness. It recommends relevant self-service, preserves safety and P1 controls, builds a complete Receiver Brief, routes to the correct queue, applies queue-owned automatic-assignment rules, supports communication on a shared timeline, and turns recurring problems into governed improvements.

ServiceNow Transition Strategy

How a production move off ServiceNow would work — without losing tickets, history, users, knowledge, SLA data, approvals, or continuity.

Proposed production approach
1. DiscoverInventory ServiceNow data and define destinations.
2. MapField, user, group, category, SLA, and approval mapping.
3. ValidateRequired-field, mapping, and duplicate checks.
4. Dry runFull validation with no writes.
5. Bulk importLoad validated records while ServiceNow stays live.
6. Delta syncIncremental sync of changes since import.
7. ReconcileCompare counts and key fields; quarantine failures.
8. Cut overRedirect new intake to MasterFlow after a short freeze.
9. StabilizeMonitor; ServiceNow read-only during validation.
10. Rollback readyControlled rollback retained until sign-off.
Technical migration approach (expand)
  • Proposed production connector: authenticated ServiceNow REST / Table API with pagination, rate-limit handling, retry, and resume-after-failure.
  • Idempotent imports keyed on ServiceNow sys_id, preserving the legacy ID and number so nothing is duplicated or lost.
  • Transformation + validation per field, with an exception quarantine for failed records and full audit logs.
  • Incremental (delta) synchronization based on ServiceNow update timestamps; last-update comparison and duplicate prevention resolve dual-run conflicts.
  • Attachment, comment/journal, approval, and SLA transfer with count reconciliation and checksum validation.
  • Rollback stops new MasterFlow intake, restores ServiceNow as the active path, reconciles the cutover window, and retries the wave — imported records are retained for audit, never mass-deleted.

Prototype boundaries

An honest distinction between what the prototype proves and what production would add.

  • No live ServiceNow API credentials — transition reporting is a modeled simulation.
  • No live corporate email integration — email intake is a documented approach.
  • Local browser storage simulates persistence; a simulated user directory backs People & Access.
  • Historical comparisons and ticket-cost history are modeled and labeled.
  • The role switcher is a demonstration control; production access comes from SSO and server-enforced permissions.
  • Classification is deterministic and local — no external AI API dependency.
  • No autonomous approval, silent duplicate merging, or mandatory auto-assignment.
  • MasterFlow is focused entirely on Problem #4 — Ticketing System Replacement.
