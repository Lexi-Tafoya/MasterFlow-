Executive summary

Employees should not need to understand a ticket catalog to ask for help.

40% Business impact

Master Electronics spends more than $300,000 per year on ServiceNow, primarily for ticket management. Employees still face a complicated intake experience: they may not know the correct request type, queue, or department, and requests often arrive without enough information for a receiver to begin work.

MasterFlow lets an employee describe the problem naturally, gathers the information the receiving team actually needs, and routes the request through the company’s existing controls. The judges care whether the project solves the problem — not how many features exist — so every part of this page traces back to that.

One continuous operational story

Requester → AI-assisted intake → Receiver → Queue & Category Manager → Governance → Intelligence.

Requester

Describes the issue naturally, answers only necessary questions, sees relevant help guidance and an honest completion estimate, and keeps communicating inside the same request.

MasterFlow intake

Classifies the request, extracts known details, measures routing and work readiness, produces a Receiver Brief, and preserves existing controls.

Receiver

Opens an actionable summary, claims or assigns work with an explainable owner recommendation, requests information, communicates with the requester, and resolves.

IT Queue & Category Manager

Manages queue health and SLA exposure, reviews assignment recommendations, spots repeated intake problems, edits owned request flows, and submits governed changes.

Megan Delia · Governance

Sets thresholds, protects routing/SLA/priority/approval/P1/safety controls, and approves or rejects enterprise-impacting changes before they publish.

MasterFlow Intelligence

Shows work readiness, classification health, returned requests, feedback patterns, flow performance, and the strongest improvement opportunity.

The winning demo sequence

Every step below works in this prototype.

  1. An employee types “Paper jam.” MasterFlow identifies the printer issue.
  2. MasterFlow asks for the exact station, and asks for scope or impact only when required.
  3. It shows routing and work readiness plus an honest typical completion estimate.
  4. A receiver-ready request is created, numbered, and visible in My Requests and the team queue.
  5. The receiver opens the Receiver Brief and requests missing information.
  6. The requester replies inside the same ticket; the timeline updates.
  7. The Queue Manager identifies a recurring intake problem and captures feedback.
  8. The Category Manager creates an improvement proposal from that feedback.
  9. Megan approves and publishes the governed change — and the actual request flow changes.
  10. Reporting shows the improvement opportunity and result.

Prototype boundaries

Stated honestly — this makes the solution more credible.

  • No production authentication; the role selector is for demonstration only.
  • No production database — browser localStorage simulates persistence.
  • No live ServiceNow migration, ERP, or Outlook integration.
  • No production AI API dependency; classification is deterministic and local.
  • No full enterprise audit engine or complex deployment workflow.
  • No autonomous approval, silent duplicate merging, or mandatory auto-assignment.
  • Duplicate awareness at intake is intentionally deferred, not claimed as working.
  • A separate, authorized Freight Optimization workspace exists but is kept out of the ticketing story.
